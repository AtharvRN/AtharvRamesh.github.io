About Me

I am a graduate student in ECE at UC San Diego and a researcher in the Trustworthy Machine Learning Lab, advised by Prof. Lily Weng.

My current work focuses on interpretability for deep learning models in medical imaging. More broadly, I am interested in AI safety, reinforcement learning, LLM post-training, and efficient inference.

Previously, I worked as an ML Engineer at Netradyne. I earned my B.Tech. in Electrical Engineering from IIT Hyderabad (EE Dept.). Selected Projects

DFlash: Accelerating LLM Inference with Speculative Decoding

Efficient inference · Speculative decoding · HF Transformers + SGLang

Extended DFlash with multi-candidate verification and end-to-end benchmarking. On AIME25, best-case throughput improved from 129.25 to 146.60 tok/s (+13.4%), with mean accepted length improving from 6.43 to 7.58.

StealthRL

AI safety · Reinforcement learning · ACL SRW under review

Built an RL paraphrase attack pipeline (GRPO + LoRA on Qwen3-4B) to evade LLM-text detectors while preserving semantics and fluency. Achieved 97.6% attack success, reducing mean TPR@1%FPR to 0.024 and AUROC from 0.79 to 0.43.

Code

Safety-Aware Speculative Decoding

Inference safety · Speculative decoding · Robustness

Designed a Composite Risk Score using draft-target disagreement, then added prompt/perplexity safeguards and 4-bit inference checks. Reduced jailbreak attack success from 12% to 4% while preserving helpfulness better than vanilla SSD.
